Art of the Day

The Japanese Footbridge, 1899

The Japanese Footbridge refers to the iconic wooden, arched bridge Claude Monet built over his water lily pond in Giverny, France, which became the subject of numerous famous Impressionist paintings, especially in the late 1890s and early 1920s, showcasing vibrant colors, reflections, and nature's harmony, reflecting his deep interest in Japanese art.

Water Lily Pond 9, 1899-1919

Water Lily Pond 9 is one of Claude Monet's iconic paintings from his Giverny garden series, specifically those depicting his Japanese-style bridge and tranquil water, showcasing his mastery of light, color, and reflection, with variations numbered by galleries or collections.
